Question 1 Method 1:
\((7-1)!\) ways of arranging the students
\(P^7_5\) ways of arranging the teachers
Total: \(6!\times P^7_5 = 6! \times \frac{7!}{2!} = 3 \times 5! \times 7!\).

Method 2:
\((5-1)!\) ways of arranging the teachers
\(P^7_5\) ways of arranging the students to separate teachers
Method 3:
Excluding the cases where at least two teachers are together.
\(P^5_2\) ways of treating two teachers as one unit.
\((7+5 - 1)! - (7+4-1)!\times P^5_2\)
