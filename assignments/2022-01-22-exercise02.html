  1. Prove that in any group with at least 2 people, there must exist at least two individuals who know the same number of people.

  2. Prove that if from a set of \(n\) integers, none of which are a multiple of \(n\), one can choose two whose difference is a multiple of \(n\).

  3. Prove that a subset of \(\{1, 2, \ldots, 2n+1\}\) with cardinality \(n+1\) has a pair of coprime elements.

  4. Consider 5 points in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) with integer coordinates. Show that there are at least two whose mid-point of the line segment joining them also have integer coordinates.

  5. Compute the Ramsey numbers \(R(1,n)\) and \(R(2,n)\) for any \(n\). Prove that \(R(m, n) = R(n,m)\).

  6. Consider the set \(\{2^1-1,2^2-1,\ldots 2^{n-1}-1\}\), where \(n\geq 3\) is odd. Prove that there is a number from this set divisible by \(n\).
